The Philosophy

Simulation is a way of learning to be human

Simulation is not merely a training tool. It is an epistemic practice — a way of trying out futures, exploring failure safely, and building shared understanding across professions and communities. When done well, simulation creates the conditions for genuine collaboration.

Participative design follows the same logic: it treats the people who live inside an institution or community as the primary experts on what needs to change. My role is to create the scaffolding — the methods, tools and facilitated space — that allows that collective intelligence to become action.

The best scenario is not the one that mirrors reality — it's the one that helps people see reality from a new angle and act together on what they find.

— Federico Monaco

Project Spotlight

SimHub — Open Medical Simulation for Everyone

SimHub is an open-source platform developed at the SimLab Laboratory of the Department of Medicine & Surgery at the University of Parma. It provides a freely accessible library of Creative Commons-licensed kits containing interactive scenarios for medical and emergency simulation.

The platform was designed to democratise access to high-quality simulation: instead of expensive mannequins and specialised spaces, it uses digital scenarios accessible from any device — making simulation available to community healthcare workers, students and citizens.

It was developed in collaboration with the Municipality of Parma's Welfare department as part of a social inclusion and digital simulation initiative — bringing simulation learning outside the hospital walls.
